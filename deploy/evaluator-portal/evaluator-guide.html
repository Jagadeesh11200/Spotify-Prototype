Evaluator guide

Does the first song come from habit, or from discovery?

This prototype tests one behavioral question for discovery-frustrated listeners: can Spotify shift the start of a session away from familiar routes by asking for present-moment emotional context before the usual home screen takes over?

Scope

This is a native Android MVP opened through BrowserStack App Live. It uses synthetic listening data, local mock recommendations, and simulated player behavior. What is under test is the interaction model: mood capture, calibrated discovery, player continuity, and adaptive re-entry.

Evaluation checkpoints, in order

Test these in sequence so the behavior changes across the MVP are easy to follow.

1
Phase 1

Baseline Spotify shell

Confirm the app still feels like Spotify: Home, Search, Library, bottom navigation, mini player, full player, likes, and familiar content surfaces.

  • Move between Home, Search, and Library using bottom navigation.
  • Start a track and open the full player.
  • Like or unlike a track and confirm the library state responds.
  • Notice that repeat behavior is easy because familiar content is always one tap away.
2
Phase 2

Mood Board into Right Now For You

Review the three-card Mood Board. The center card is the primary choice, while side cards preview alternate emotional worlds. The screen now uses a lighter Spotify-style dark background, a green Go to Home pill, animated side swipe hints, and a blinking theme-colored Tap to enter cue. Each Mood Board session narrows the full 15-world library down to exactly three personalized cards using time of day, recent mood history, exit behavior, novelty, and emotional distance. That trio stays consistent while the app session is open, including when the user returns from the Home vibe chip or Tune card; a fresh app launch generates a new trio.

  • Confirm the screen only presents three personalized mood cards in the current session, not the full 15-world library.
  • All three mood worlds should feel distinct in color, atmosphere, fragment language, and top mood icon.
  • The center card should show the atmosphere, fragment, memory anchor, and community count clearly.
  • Use the side swipe hints as the cue to move across cards, then tap a side card to bring it into the center.
  • Return to the Mood Board from Tune, the vibe chip, or the compact tile; the same three-card set should remain available during that app session.
  • Tap the center card or the blinking Tap to enter text to select that mood.
  • Tap one of the preview songs at the top of a card; it should enter that mood and start that song automatically.
  • Confirm that Home opens with the active mood visible and "Right Now For You" above regular sections.
  • The "Right Now For You" header should show the selected mood icon beside the title; individual song tiles should not have mood badges.
  • Play a recommendation and confirm it feeds into the normal player flow.
3
Phase 3

Change, cancel, and tune again

After a mood is selected, Home keeps discovery flexible instead of locking the user into that choice. This is the path shown by the active vibe chip and the mood tuning card.

  • Find the active Vibe: selected mood chip near the top of Home.
  • Tap the chip body to reopen the Mood Board and change to a different mood.
  • Use the small close/cancel button on the chip to clear the selected mood.
  • After clearing, confirm the "How is your mood today?" card appears with the resonance sessions label.
  • Tap Tune on that card and confirm the Mood Board opens again.
  • Select a new card and confirm "Right Now For You" returns with refreshed recommendations.
4
Phase 4

Exit memory and compact entry

This phase tests whether the discovery prompt can back off when a user repeatedly skips it, then return when they actively choose a mood.

  • From the Mood Board, tap the green Go to Home pill without selecting a card five times in a row.
  • On the fifth exit, Home should show a compact persistent mood tile instead of the full intercept.
  • Tap the tile to reopen the full triptych.
  • Select a card and confirm the app reaches "Right Now For You" normally.
  • After selection, the exit count resets and the full Mood Board can return on the next fresh session.

The 90-second script

  1. Open Mobile Session and inspect the three-card Mood Board, including the mood icon, swipe hints, and blinking "Tap to enter" cue.
  2. Swap a side card into the center, then tap the center card or "Tap to enter".
  3. Restart the Mood Board and tap a preview song to confirm the mood is entered and playback starts automatically.
  4. Confirm "Right Now For You" appears above normal Home content with the selected mood icon beside the section title.
  5. Tap the active Vibe chip to change mood, then use the chip close button to clear the mood.
  6. Tap the Tune button on the "How is your mood today?" card and select a new mood.
  7. Return to Mood Board and use the green "Go to Home" pill five times to trigger the compact tile.
  8. Tap the tile, select a mood, and confirm the full recommendation flow still works.
